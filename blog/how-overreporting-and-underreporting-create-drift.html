How Overreporting And Underreporting Create Drift

Too much information and too little information can both move the project away from the exact question.

NoDrift conceptReporting discipline
User problemThe AI agent reports in a way that hides the exact state.
Reading depthPractical

The problem

Overreporting buries the answer inside extra explanation. Underreporting leaves out the facts the user needs. Both can make the user work harder to understand what actually happened.

Why this causes drift

When the report is not exact, the next turn starts from a distorted picture. The AI agent may then solve a problem that was created by unclear reporting rather than the real project problem.

How NoDrift helps

NoDrift pushes the AI agent toward bounded reporting: what changed, what was checked, what remains open, and what exact approval is needed next.

Practical hint

Ask for the report shape you need: "Give me only the result, the changed files, the verification, and the next approval gate." That keeps the answer useful without letting it sprawl.
