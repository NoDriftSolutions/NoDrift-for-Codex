Approval gates

Why A Casual Yes Should Not Mean Everything

A small approval should stay attached to the actual thing the user meant, not expand into a wider action or future permission for the AI agent.

NoDrift conceptBounded approval
User problemA small yes becomes broad permission for the AI agent.
Reading depthFoundation

The problem

Agentic AI work can move quickly. A user says yes to one thing, and the AI agent may treat that answer as permission for several related actions.

That can feel efficient until it crosses a line: a draft becomes a file edit, a local change becomes a public claim, or a discussion becomes implementation.

Bounded approval

Bounded approval means the approval belongs to the exact action, object, destination, and limit that were stated. It does not quietly authorize adjacent work.

Example

"Yes, draft the outline" is not approval to publish the post. "Go create the local blog pages" is not approval to push the site to GitHub. "Fix this paragraph" is not approval to rewrite the whole page.

NoDrift's role

NoDrift sets reception-side workspace rules that prevent the AI agent from treating a narrow user approval as broad permission.

Practical hint

Before an important action, ask for the exact bounded task in one sentence. If the sentence is broader than you intended, correct it before saying yes.
