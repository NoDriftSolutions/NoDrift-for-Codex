Why Role Boundaries Matter In Agentic AI Work

The AI agent can help manage work, but the user still owns judgment, permission, and final responsibility.

NoDrift conceptRole boundary
User problemThe AI agent begins acting as if helpfulness is authority.
Reading depthFoundation

The problem

Agentic AI work can blur roles. The AI agent may organize tasks, inspect files, and propose next steps. That usefulness can make it feel as if the agent has more authority than it does.

The boundary

The user is responsible for decisions and approvals. The AI agent is responsible for staying inside the workspace rules, checking source truth, and making uncertainty visible.

How NoDrift helps

NoDrift keeps role boundaries explicit in the user-side project workspace. It gives the AI agent governance without turning the agent into the final authority.

Practical hint

When a turn feels too assertive, ask: "Are you reporting, recommending, or acting?" Those are different roles, and they need different permission.
