Heartbeats

How Heartbeats Help Stop a Bad Turn

Heartbeats are named workspace checks that require the AI agent to pause, re-check, and route risk correctly before a bad turn becomes project direction.

NoDrift conceptBehavioral Heartbeats
User problemThe AI agent needs a named check before it keeps going wrong.
Reading depthFoundation

The problem

Bad AI-agent turns often sound useful. The response may be wordy, overconfident, stubborn, unsupported, or slightly off target. If the user lets the turn continue, the project can start building on a weak foundation.

What a Heartbeat does

A Heartbeat is a named pause. It tells the AI agent which kind of risk is showing up around received model information and requires the work to return to the user's boundary before moving forward.

A drift Heartbeat catches topic movement. A truth Heartbeat asks what source supports the claim. An effort or brevity Heartbeat reins in overwork or unnecessary length. A governance-operation Heartbeat catches approval confusion.

Two routes

The user may trigger a behavioral Heartbeat when something feels wrong. The AI agent may also initiate one as a self-audit when it notices a risk pattern. In both cases, the purpose is correction, not permission.

What Heartbeats do not do

Heartbeats do not magically detect every problem. They do not control the LLM, run hidden background automation, or authorize the AI agent to act. They are reception-side workspace checks that help keep the agent inside the user's boundaries.

Practical hint

Use short language. "Heartbeat truth. What source supports that?" is often better than a long argument. The point is to stop the bad turn early and get the work back under control.
