Why Literal Communication Matters With LLM Information

Literal communication keeps agentic AI work tied to the exact object instead of letting the conversation slide into nearby meaning.

NoDrift conceptLiteral communication standard
User problemThe AI agent treats implied meaning as permission or direction.
Reading depthPractical

The problem

received model information often sounds complete even when it contains assumptions. If the AI agent treats those assumptions as facts, the project can move away from the user's exact intent.

Literal means exact

Literal communication asks what was actually said, what was not said, and what still needs approval. It does not reward implied permission, soft hints, or confident guesses.

How NoDrift helps

NoDrift gives the user-side project workspace a stricter communication standard. The AI agent should handle received model information as material to classify, not as authority to expand the task.

Practical hint

Use exact object language: "Answer this question only," "Do not widen the task," "This is a draft," or "No action is approved." Good boundaries reduce repair work.
