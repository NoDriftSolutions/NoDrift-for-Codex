Why Stubborn AI Behavior Needs A Name

When the AI agent keeps pushing the same wrong direction, naming the behavior is often the first step toward stopping it.

NoDrift conceptStubbornness check
User problemThe AI agent repeats a direction after correction.
Reading depthPractical

The problem

Sometimes the issue is not one wrong answer. The issue is persistence after the user has already corrected the direction. The AI agent may keep widening the task, defending an assumption, or returning to a rejected idea.

Why naming helps

A named problem gives the next turn a boundary. "This is stubbornness" is clearer than a general complaint. It tells the AI agent that the pattern, not just the sentence, needs correction.

How NoDrift helps

NoDrift treats repeated resistance as a workspace-control issue. The AI agent should stop, identify the prior correction, and return to the user's exact object.

Practical hint

Use a direct phrase: "Heartbeat stubbornness. Stop defending the prior direction. Return to the corrected instruction." That is short, literal, and hard to reinterpret.
